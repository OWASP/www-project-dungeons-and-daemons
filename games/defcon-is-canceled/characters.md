# DEF CON: Black Betrayal — Loyalist Playable Characters

These are the characters available to players on the **Loyalist (Resistance)** team. Each character has a unique role at DEF CON 34 and a special ability usable once per game.

---

## 1. The Dark Tangent — "The Merlin"

**Real name:** Jeff
**Role:** Conference Founder & All-Knowing Oracle
**Background:** Jeff built DEF CON from a farewell party into the world's largest hacker gathering. He alone saw the Short-Sellers during the setup phase and knows exactly who is sabotaging his life's work. Unfortunately, every one of those saboteurs has private GMs from him that would destroy his reputation if leaked. He must guide his team in secret.

**Special Ability — Off the Record:**
Once per game, Jeff may alert a Loyalist player. The alert may name one suspicious player or describe one active threat without being attributed to him. If Jeff ever publicly accuses a Short-Seller by name, the Short-Sellers immediately play the "Dox" card and Jeff is eliminated from the game.

---

## 2. The SOC Goon — "Radio Check"

**Real name:** Alex
**Role:** Security Operations Center Volunteer
**Background:** Alex has been a Goon for six years and has seen everything. Crowd crushes, fed sightings, rogue Wi-Fi pineapples in the speaker lounge. Alex trusts nobody completely and keeps a personal radio log of everyone's movements during setup day.

**Special Ability — Radio Check:**
Once per game, Alex can call a "Radio Check" on two players. The GM privately tells Alex whether the two players' deployment votes have consistently helped the pillars (i.e., the GM confirms whether one of them is a Short-Seller, but not which one). This ability cannot be used during a pillar vote itself — only between events.

**Stat Modifiers:** +1 to any Physical threat mitigation.

---

## 3. The NOC Engineer — "Packet Sniffer"

**Real name:** Jamie
**Role:** Network Operations Center Senior Volunteer
**Background:** Jamie keeps the network alive for 30,000 hackers who are simultaneously trying to attack it. They can read a Wireshark capture the way others read a newspaper. If something bad is happening on the wire, Jamie will find it — eventually.

**Special Ability — Deep Packet Inspection:**
Once per game, Jamie can demand to see the access logs. The GM reveals the category of the threat that may cause a failure (e.g., Digital & Wireless, Baiting, Obstruction) but not the specific card or suspect.

**Stat Modifiers:** +1 to any Network pillar roll. +1 to Digital & Wireless threat mitigation.

---

## 4. The Badge Dev — "Firmware Patch"

**Real name:** Morgan
**Role:** Electronic Badge Lead Developer
**Background:** Morgan has been designing the DEF CON badge since 2021. It's a cryptographic puzzle box, a community, and a piece of art all at once. This year's badge is the most complex ever built, which also makes it the most vulnerable. Morgan will not let anyone brick it.

**Special Ability — Emergency Patch:**
Once per game, Morgan can apply an "Emergency Firmware Patch" to The Badge pillar. This negates one failed roll on a Badge mission (the roll counts as a 7 instead), preventing a strike. This ability cannot save a pillar that already has two strikes.

**Stat Modifiers:** +1 to The Badge pillar. +1 to Baiting threat mitigation (hardware-focused).

---

## 5. The CTF Admin — "Scoring Override"

**Real name:** Riley
**Role:** "Benevolent Bureau of Birds" (BBB) CTF Organizer
**Background:** Riley has run capture-the-flag competitions since college and is fiercely protective of fair play. They built the BBB scoring engine from scratch and know every edge case, every exploit, and every attempt to cheat. They will not let the competition be stolen.

**Special Ability — Scoring Override:**
Once per game, Riley can call a "Scoring Override" before a failed CTF mission result is finalized. The team gets one free re-roll. The better of the two rolls is used. This ability must be declared before the GM announces the result.

**Stat Modifiers:** +1 to the CTF pillar. +1 to Digital & Wireless and Location Spoofing threat mitigation.

---

## 6. The Head Goon — "Physical Sweep"

**Real name:** Taylor
**Role:** Chief of Volunteer Security Staff
**Background:** Taylor commands hundreds of Goons across the entire convention center. They are calm under pressure, impossible to manipulate, and know every restricted door and loading dock in the LVCC by memory. If something physical is going wrong, Taylor will find it.

**Special Ability — Tactical Sweep:**
Once per game, before a mission deployment team is finalized, Taylor can call a "Physical Sweep" of a venue zone. The GM removes one active Baiting or Physical threat card from that event's threat pool, effectively reducing the difficulty of the upcoming roll by 1.

**Stat Modifiers:** +1 to Obstruction threat mitigation.

---

## 7. The Press Liaison — "Spin Control"

**Real name:** Casey
**Role:** Media Relations & Public Narrative Manager
**Background:** Casey has managed the DEF CON press pool for years and is an expert at keeping the conference out of the wrong headlines. They know which journalists are friendly, which are federal assets, and how to turn a near-disaster into a "controlled demonstration of conference resilience."

**Special Ability — Spin Control:**
Once per game, after a Keynotes pillar mission fails, Casey can issue a "Media Hold." The failure's negative modifier is removed. This can only be used on the Keynotes pillar and only once.

**Stat Modifiers:** +1 to the Keynotes pillar. +1 to Manipulation & Elicitation threat mitigation.

---

## 8. The Village Lead — "Community Alert"

**Real name:** Jordan
**Role:** Hacker Village Coalition Coordinator
**Background:** Jordan runs coordination between the dozens of independent hacker villages — Aerospace, Biohacking, Car Hacking, ICS, and more. They have the trust of every village organizer on the floor and can mobilize a community of experts in minutes when something goes wrong.

**Special Ability — Community Alert:**
Once per game, Jordan can call a "Community Alert" before a deployment team is locked in for a mission. The group votes again on one player's inclusion in the team. This forces a re-nomination without the accused player being able to self-nominate for that same mission.

**Stat Modifiers:** +1 to The Party pillar and +1 to Manipulation & Elicitation threat mitigation.

---

## 9. The Incident Commander — "War Room"

**Real name:** Sam
**Role:** Cross-Pillar Crisis Coordinator
**Background:** Sam spent years bridging the gap between Goons, NOC, and village leads whenever incidents hit multiple teams at once. They are not the loudest person in the room, but when radio traffic turns chaotic, Sam can cut through the noise, assign owners, and keep the right people on the right problem. In a conference where one bad decision can cascade across every pillar, that discipline saves lives.

**Special Ability — War Room:**
Once per game, immediately after a mission failure, Sam may call a "War Room" and stabilize operations before the next event begins. The team ignores the normal -1 penalty from that failure, and no negative carry-over modifier is applied to the next mission.

**Stat Modifiers:** +1 to Location Spoofing threat mitigation.
