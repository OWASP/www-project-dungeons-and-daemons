# DEF CON: Black Betrayal — Non-Player Characters (NPCs)

NPCs are characters controlled by the Dungeon Master. Loyalists can interact with NPCs between mission events to gather intel, report threats, or call in favors. NPCs do not vote on deployment teams.

---

## Authority & Venue NPCs

These NPCs represent institutions and can shut down or save Core Pillars depending on how Loyalists interact with them.

---

### Director Henderson — FBI Liaison

**Role:** Federal Bureau of Investigation Field Liaison, DEF CON Observer Program
**Appearance:** Mid-50s, pressed shirt under an ill-fitting sport coat, lanyard that says "OBSERVER." Always nursing a terrible cup of convention-center coffee.

**Personality:** Gruff but not hostile. Henderson has been attending DEF CON under the FBI's "Feds Attack, Feds Defend" (FA/FD) agreement for years. He respects the community but will act if a genuine federal crime is in progress.

**What he knows:** Henderson is aware of unusual Polymarket activity around DEF CON's cancellation. He hasn't connected it to anyone specific yet.

**How Loyalists can use Henderson:**
- Reporting a confirmed National Security or FCC-level threat to Henderson removes that threat from the active pool for that event (DM discretion).
- However, if Loyalists report a threat that turns out to be wrong, Henderson becomes suspicious of the Loyalists and applies a −1 modifier to the next Keynotes mission (the feds start scrutinizing the event itself).

**Sample Dialog:**
> "Look, I'm not here to bust anyone for running a packet sniffer. But if someone is genuinely interfering with aviation signals or medical devices, that's not a hacker game anymore. You bring me something real, I'll make a call."

---

### Patricia Vasquez — LVCC Venue Manager

**Role:** Las Vegas Convention Center, West Hall Operations Director
**Appearance:** Early 40s, always in a blazer, headset perpetually looped around one ear, tablet in hand.

**Personality:** Professional, firm, and deeply risk-averse. Patricia has managed conventions for fifteen years and her first instinct in any crisis is to protect the venue, not the event. She likes DEF CON but the moment it becomes a liability she will pull the plug.

**What she controls:** Patricia can invoke a "Venue Hold" that counts as an automatic strike against any active Core Pillar. She can also unlock restricted areas or provide official LVCC staff assistance if properly persuaded.

**How Loyalists can use Vasquez:**
- Reporting a verified Physical or Location Spoofing threat to Vasquez before it triggers earns the team a +1 modifier on the next physical event roll.
- If Loyalists fail to address a threat that results in a crowd safety incident (Fire Marshal Shutdown, Stampede), Vasquez applies a Venue Hold and adds −2 to the next mission modifier.

**Sample Dialog:**
> "I need to know right now: is this a stunt or an actual safety issue? Because I have the Fire Marshal on speed dial and he is not a fan of hacker conferences to begin with. Help me, help you."

---

### Fire Marshal Rodriguez — Clark County Fire & Life Safety

**Role:** Clark County Fire Department, Convention Center Inspection & Compliance Officer
**Appearance:** Late 50s, perpetually unimpressed, clipboard always in hand. Wears a fire department windbreaker over civilian clothes.

**Personality:** By-the-book, humorless, and genuinely competent. Rodriguez does not dislike DEF CON personally — he dislikes unsafe conditions. He will not be argued with, bribed, or charmed. He responds only to facts.

**What he controls:** Rodriguez has the authority to clear and close any section of the West Hall. Three fire-safety violations (crowd obstruction, false alarms, fire-hazard bait devices counted by the DM as "Rodriguez Events") will cause Rodriguez to shut down the entire conference, ending the game as a Short-Seller victory.

**How Loyalists can use Rodriguez:**
- Loyalists cannot charm Rodriguez, but they can get ahead of a threat. If a player correctly identifies and reports a Physical or Baiting fire-hazard threat to the DM and it matches the active threat card, Rodriguez is notified "proactively." This counts as a successful mitigation with no roll required — once per game.

**Sample Dialog:**
> "I don't care what year the conference is. If that exit is blocked, I clear the room. No exceptions. You have ninety seconds to show me it's clear or I'm calling it."

---

### Dr. Chen — Medical & HAZMAT Team Lead

**Role:** On-site Emergency Medical Director, DEF CON Medical Staff
**Appearance:** Early 40s, calm to the point of seeming bored, always wearing scrubs. Carries a trauma kit.

**Personality:** Methodical and unflappable. Dr. Chen has treated everything from badge-soldering burns to serious medical emergencies at DEF CON. She is a loyalist by nature — she wants the con to run — but she will call a halt if attendee safety is genuinely at risk.

**What she controls:** Dr. Chen can neutralize a Baiting (chemical/biological) or Obstruction (medical lane blockage) threat card if Loyalists report it to her. This counts as a free mitigation for that specific threat type, once per game.

**Sample Dialog:**
> "If someone left unknown compounds in a village area, I need to know in the next five minutes. I'm not shutting anything down — I just need to look at it first. If it's nothing, great. If it's something, we handle it quietly."

---

### "Darknet Dave" Kowalski — Underground Tech Journalist

**Role:** Freelance reporter, contributor to several pseudonymous infosec publications
**Appearance:** 30s, ironic t-shirt, press badge that may or may not be real, always recording on his phone.

**Personality:** Dave is not malicious but he is reckless. He wants the scoop and he doesn't care much about collateral damage. He could be a tool or a threat depending on how Loyalists handle him.

**What he knows:** Dave has heard rumors about the Polymarket bets. He doesn't know who is behind them but he's been asking around.

**How Loyalists can use Dave:**
- If Loyalists feed Dave a confirmed true story about Short-Seller activity, Dave publishes it. The Short-Sellers lose one "stealth token" — the DM reveals which threat category they have been using most frequently.
- If Loyalists feed Dave a false tip (to misdirect), and it turns out to be false, Dave publishes a retraction that embarrasses the conference. −1 modifier to the next Keynotes mission.

**Sample Dialog:**
> "Off the record? Something is very wrong with the money flow here. Someone wants this con dead. I just need one name — one verifiable thing — and I go public. You give me something real, I'll protect your source."

## The Short-Sellers — NPC Antagonists

The Short-Sellers are the hidden antagonists. In gameplay, some of these roles are secretly assigned to players. However, for games with fewer players or when the DM needs to represent an unassigned character, these NPCs appear as background actors at the conference. Loyalists can attempt to investigate them between events.

**DM Note:** If a Loyalist spends their between-event action to "shadow" or investigate one of the named Short-Sellers below, roll a d6. On a 4+, the DM gives a vague clue about which threat category that character is associated with. On a 1–3, the character notices and the DM applies a −1 modifier to the next mission (the Short-Seller now knows they are being watched and is more careful).
